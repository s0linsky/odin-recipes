Description

Wagyu is any of the four Japanese breeds of beef cattle. In several areas of Japan, Wagyu beef is shipped carrying area names. Some examples are Matsusaka beef, Kobe beef, Yonezawa beef, Mishima beef, ÅŒmi beef, and Sanda beef

Ingredients

  • Wagyu Steak
  • Salt & Pepper, To Taste

Steps

  1. Rest: Rest your steak at room temperature 30 minutes to an hour before cooking. This creates an even temperature in the steak allowing you to cook it evenly.
  2. Salt: Salt steak with Kosher salt on both sides. The salt draws out some of the fat and juices of the steak to create a delicious, flavorful crust.
  3. Heat: Take your cast iron pan or stainless steel skillet and put it on the stove at medium heat. Allow the pan to get hot.
  4. Grease: Rub the fat cap around the steak on the entire inside of the pan. The fat will begin to melt and "grease" the pan to prevent the steak from sticking.
  5. Cook: Place the steak on the hot cast iron or stainless steel pan and cook for about a minute and half.
  6. Flip: Flip the steak and cook until the internal temperature reaches about 120 to 125 degrees for rare or 130 to 135 degrees for medium rare.
  7. Rest: Rest 5 to 10 minutes then slice into strips.
